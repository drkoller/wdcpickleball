DCPL: The Story

A community created through resilience and volunteer effort.

DCPL was born from a simple problem: there were not enough places to play pickleball in Washington, DC.

In 2022, pickleball options in DC were limited—a few recreation centers with restricted hours, very few outdoor courts, and only a handful of places where players could regularly gather.

Steve, one of DC’s original pickleball players, had spent nearly two decades playing, competing in tournaments, and helping grow the sport.

Maya joined those efforts as a volunteer, and together they began building a community around the game.

In Spring 2022, Maya was looking for a wall to hit against near her neighborhood. That brought her to East Potomac Tennis Center.

A conversation about pickleball revealed an opportunity: using underused tennis space to create more places for the growing pickleball community.

In Fall 2022, Steve and Maya began hosting volunteer open play at the EPTC bubble three nights a week without dedicated pickleball courts.

They taped courts for each session, organized players, and created a hub where groups from DC, Virginia, and Maryland could come together to play, improve, and connect.

As the community grew, the need for a better way to match players by skill level became clear.

David joined the effort, bringing his background as a computer scientist and problem solver.

David created DCPL’s ladder and algorithm, helping players find competitive, well-matched games.

Together, Steve, Maya, and David founded DCPL in April 2023.

Since then, DCPL has grown into a community with leagues, tournaments, clinics, open plays, and community events.

What makes DCPL different is the people behind it—a community built through passion, resilience, and connection.
